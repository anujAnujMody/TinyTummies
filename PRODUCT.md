# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Parents (primarily mothers) of 4–12 year olds in urban India. They are time-pressed, health-conscious, and exhausted by the daily negotiation of getting their child to eat balanced meals. The emotional context is guilt and stress — wasted food, rushed mornings, worry about nutrition. They discover the service through WhatsApp groups, school networks, or social media.

## Product Purpose

Tiny Tummies solves the school lunch problem: fresh, balanced, 100% vegetarian meals delivered to school Monday to Saturday. Parents subscribe to weekly or monthly plans. Kids get food they actually want to eat. The service removes guilt, saves time, and ensures consistent nutrition. Success = a parent who never packs a tiffin again.

## Brand Personality

Fun, vibrant, playful, trustworthy.

The brand speaks to two audiences simultaneously: kids (delight, excitement, "I want that!") and parents (confidence, relief, "this is safe and nutritious"). Tone is warm, conversational, slightly mischievous — never clinical or corporate.

Emotional goals:
- **Parents**: relief, trust, confidence
- **Kids**: excitement, curiosity, delight

## Anti-references

- Dull, corporate, or sterile designs (hospital, clinic, or government-school feel)
- Generic SaaS landing page templates (gradient text, identical card grids, glassmorphism)
- Overly babyish or infantile aesthetics (this is for school-age kids, not toddlers)
- Dark, moody, or serious food photography
- Anything that looks like a nutrition textbook or diet plan

## Reference Brands

- **Little Spoon** — playful DTC kids food, bold creative choices, joyful naming
- **Ella's Kitchen** — childlike illustrations, sensory play, foodie onomatopoeia
- **Copow** — colorful meal delivery, "eat with your whole face" energy
- **Feastables** — wild, animated, unapologetically fun
- **Graza** — hand-drawn, whimsical, anti-corporate in a traditional category

## Design Principles

1. **Food should look alive** — color, texture, and movement make nutrition feel exciting, not dutiful
2. **Two voices, one page** — speak to the kid's desire and the parent's judgment simultaneously
3. **Show, don't lecture** — no nutrition charts, no ingredient lists as the hero. Let the food and the joy sell
4. **Warmth over polish** — hand-drawn, slightly imperfect, human. Not a tech startup
5. **Trust through specificity** — real details (₹68/meal, Monday-Saturday, 100% vegetarian) beat vague promises

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum for all text
- Support reduced motion preferences
- Color choices must work for common color blindness (avoid red/green as sole differentiators)
- Hindi/vernacular support considered for future
